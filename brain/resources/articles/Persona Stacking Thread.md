#### Key Details
- How to build your own persona stack:
	1. Identify the core skill needed (copywriter, analyst, etc.) 
	2. Add perspective layers: • Psychology (human behavior) • Data/Economics (numbers) • Systems thinking (connections) 
	3. Add a differentiator: • Contrarian (challenges norms) • Storyteller (narrative) • Educator (clarity) 
	4. Mix 2-4 personas max. More = diluted.


Common persona stacking mistakes:
-  Too many personas (5+) = confused output
- Conflicting personas (creative + rigid analyst)  
- Vague personas ("expert" vs "behavioral psychologist")
- No clear task after persona definition

Wins:
-  2-3 complementary personas
- Specific titles
- Clear objective after persona stack



1/ Content Creation Personas: Copywriter + Behavioral Psychologist + Data Analyst Prompt: "Act as a copywriter who understands behavioral psychology and data-driven content strategy. Write a LinkedIn post about [topic] that triggers curiosity, uses pattern interrupts, and optimizes for engagement metrics." Result: Content that hooks AND converts.

2/ Product Strategy Personas: Product Manager + UX Designer + Economist Prompt: "Act as a product manager with UX design expertise and economic modeling skills. Analyze this feature request considering user experience, development costs, and market positioning. What's the ROI?" Result: Decisions backed by multiple frameworks.

3/ Sales & Persuasion Personas: Sales Expert + Psychologist + Storyteller Prompt: "Act as a sales expert who understands psychological triggers and storytelling. Write a cold email for [product] that uses reciprocity, social proof, and narrative structure to book meetings." Result: Emails that actually get responses (tested: +156% reply rate).


4/ Business Analysis Personas: Management Consultant + Financial Analyst + Industry Expert Prompt: "Act as a management consultant with financial analysis expertise in [industry]. Evaluate this business opportunity using Porter's Five Forces, unit economics, and industry-specific risks." Result: MBA-level analysis in minutes.


5/ Learning & Education Personas: Teacher + Cognitive Scientist + Subject Expert Prompt: "Act as an expert teacher who understands cognitive load theory and learning science. Explain [complex topic] using spaced repetition principles, analogies, and progressive complexity." Result: You actually understand AND remember it.

6/ Creative Problem Solving Personas: Innovation Consultant + Systems Thinker + Contrarian Prompt: "Act as an innovation consultant who thinks in systems and challenges assumptions. Solve [problem] by: (1) identifying hidden constraints, (2) proposing contrarian solutions, (3) mapping second-order effects." Result: Solutions nobody else sees.

7/ Technical Communication Personas: Engineer + Technical Writer + Educator Prompt: "Act as a senior engineer who excels at technical writing and teaching. Explain [technical concept] with: correct terminology, clear analogies for non-technical readers, and practical code examples." Result: Technical depth without the jargon barrier.
