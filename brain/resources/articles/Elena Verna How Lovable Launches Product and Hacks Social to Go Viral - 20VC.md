# Elena Verna: How Lovable Launches Product & Hacks Social to Go Viral

## Summary

Elena Verna (Head of Growth at Lovable) breaks down how Lovable reached $350M+ ARR and $6.6B valuation. Key insights on why growth is now a trust problem, why founder/employee social presence beats traditional marketing, and why SEO is table stakes but won't win you the market.

## Key Stats

- **Lovable ARR:** $350M+
- **Valuation:** $6.6B
- **Growth philosophy:** Trust-first, emotion-driven, social-native

## How Growth Is Changing in AI

### 1. Growth Is Now a Trust Problem

As software creation democratizes, functionality is table stakes. The question becomes:
- Who do I trust to purchase from?
- Who do I trust to use?
- Do I believe in the team building it?

**If users don't trust you, they'll just build it themselves.**

### 2. Minimum Lovable Product (MLP)

Software is now judged by **emotion**, not just functionality.

**Maslov's pyramid analogy:**
- Bottom layer: It works, it's secure
- Next layer: I can connect to it
- Top layer: It has personality

**Humans don't like utilities. We want connection.**

### 3. Performance Marketing Is Getting Automated

- Channel optimizations are being automated
- Growth work = innovation, creative campaigns
- Goal: Capture hearts and minds, not just optimize funnels

## Channel Strategy

### What Becomes MORE Important

**Your product as a channel** — earning trust, driving word of mouth, creating likability

**Social (but not traditional social):**
- Not company account posting puns
- **Founder-led socials**
- **Employee-led socials**
- **Building in public**

> "Encourage your employees to build in public and grow their social followings. Reach your customer through your own team's voices."

### What Becomes LESS Important

Traditional marketing/sales techniques — the old playbook of driving growth through conventional channels.

## On SEO

**Is SEO dying?**

> "It's declining. The habits are changing absolutely. But is SEO still fruitful? Abso-fucking-lutely. You still have to invest in SEO if you want to have the baseline of growth. But is SEO going to be the reason that you're going to win in your business? I don't think so."

**Verdict:** SEO is table stakes, not a differentiator.

## On Anton's Founder Brand

**How important was Anton's social presence to Lovable's early traction?**

> "I think Lovable is where it is right now because of the founder socials that Anton has really leaned into... At the beginning, was he the reason that Lovable spiked and got to the traction that allowed it to scale where it is right now? I think so."

**Current state:** Less important now because they've diversified, but still meaningful.

## Channel Diversification Strategy

**When to diversify:**
- When you have one channel working
- Before you become over-reliant on a single point of failure

**Lovable's approach:**
- Started with Anton's social (single point of failure)
- Diversified to employee socials, community, UGC
- Anton still growing, still important, but not the only channel

## The "Build In Public" Objection

**Founder concern:** "If I encourage employees to build personal brands, they'll get hired away."

**Elena's response:**

1. **If employees are easily swayed by a better offer, you have cultural issues.** Look at your recruiting practices — are you hiring warm bodies or mission-aligned people?

2. **Use the company to help people build social presence.** This becomes a recruiting/retention tool, not a liability.

## Key Quotes

> "Growth is a trust problem now."

> "Software is now almost being judged by the emotion that it can invoke with a human as opposed to just core basic functionality."

> "We as humans always want to connect with something. We don't like utilities. We don't like tools."

> "I really mean where you start building in public and you encourage your employees to build in public... reach your customer through your own team's voices."

> "Is SEO still fruitful? Abso-fucking-lutely. Like you still have to invest in SEO... But is SEO going to be the reason that you're going to win in your business? I don't think so."

> "If you think that your employees are so easily swayed to go somewhere else just if they get reached out by the right company, what are you doing? Like there is some really bad cultural issues in your company."
