# The Backbone of a Successful Startup: Operations

## Summary

Zach Cusimano (13 years in tech, built People.ai to $1.1B) explains why operations is the backbone of successful startups, using a sports coaching analogy to illustrate how ops teams align and enable the entire organization.

## The Sports Analogy

**The Championship = Your Customers**  
**Jordan & Pippen = Sales & Engineering**  
**Phil Jackson = Operations** — designing plays, holding the stopwatch, screaming at the ref

## Operations' Two Primary Objectives

1. **Planning** — Strategy, alignment, goal setting, monitoring
2. **Action** — Services (next: what is a Service?)

## The Modern Ops Landscape

Ops has expanded far beyond traditional boundaries:
- Rev Ops
- Dev Ops
- Product Ops
- Legal Ops
- People Ops
- Finance Ops

## Key Insights

- Ops is the "connective tissue" between teams
- When it matters most, ops makes or breaks you
- Good ops teams align everyone toward common goals
- Scope of "ops" is broad and growing

## Key Quotes

> "Consider your favorite team's coaching staff... Coaches align the team toward a common goal, guide the flow of the game, recruit the best players, maximize their strengths, they know the score and keep an eye on the clock. Business Operations is the same."

> "Your Customers are the NBA Championship. Sales and Engineering are Jordan and Pippen. Operations is Phil Jackson."
