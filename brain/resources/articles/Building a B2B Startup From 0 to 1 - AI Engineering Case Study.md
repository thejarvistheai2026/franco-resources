# Building a B2B Startup From 0 to 1: AI Engineering Case Study

## Summary

Sid Ramesh shares the journey of building a B2B AI startup from 0 to 1 — landing enterprise customers, generating $2M+ ARR, and the lessons learned along the way. Covers everything from initial customer discovery to team building and the mistakes that almost killed the company.

## The Starting Point

**Year 1:** Founded company with thesis around applied AI. No product, no customers, just conviction that vertical AI would reshape industries.

**The challenge:** Everyone was building horizontal AI tools. The thesis was that vertical AI — deeply trained on specific industry data and workflows — would win.

**The first 6 months:** Pure customer discovery. 100+ conversations with operators in target industry. Not selling. Just listening.

## Finding the Problem

**The insight:** Every enterprise had the same pain point.

- Data scattered across 15+ systems
- No way to query it conversationally
- Existing "AI solutions" were bolted-on horizontal tools that didn't understand industry context
- Compliance requirements made generic AI unusable

**The key:** Found a workflow where AI could deliver 10x improvement, not just 10%.

## Building the MVP

**Time to first customer:** 8 months

**What we built:**
- Not a platform
- Not a "solution"
- A very specific workflow automation that saved 40 hours per week for one team

**The scope:**
- 4 data integrations (not 40)
- 3 specific use cases (not unlimited)
- 1 customer vertical (not horizontal)

**The first customer:**
- Landed via warm intro from discovery calls
- Paid $50K pilot (structured as service, not software)
- Success criteria: Prove 30 hour/week savings
- Actually delivered: 45 hour/week savings

## The Pivot Moment

**Month 10:** Almost died.

**The mistake:**
- First customer success convinced us to go horizontal
- Built platform before nailing second customer
- Started burning $150K/month
- Team size: 18 people

**The correction:**
- Cut team to 5 people
- Focussed exclusively on proving repeatable motion in one vertical
- Became "the AI vendor for X industry"
- Not "AI for everyone"

**The lesson:** Do things that don't scale until they do. Platform thinking is death before product-market fit.

## Scaling to $2M ARR

**Year 2 metrics:**
- 8 customers
- $2.1M ARR
- $175K ACV
- 95% retention
- 0 churn

**How:**

### 1. Reference Selling
- First 3 customers came from intros
- Required obsessive customer success
- Reference calls were sales pipeline
- Customer logos were better than marketing

### 2. Land and Expand
- Initial deployment: 10 user seats
- Month 6: 50 seats
- Month 18: 200 seats, enterprise-wide contract

**Expansion playbook:**
- Start with one team, one use case
- Prove value, document ROI
- Expand to adjacent teams
- Eventually become infrastructure

### 3. Founder-led Sales
- First $500K: Founder did every sales call
- Present at every implementation
- Knew every customer by name
- Responded to every support ticket (initially)

**The shift:** Only hired sales team after $1M ARR and repeatable playbook.

## The Technology That Actually Mattered

**What we built:**
- RAG over enterprise data
- Industry-specific fine-tuning
- Compliance layer (SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.)

**What we didn't build:**
- Proprietary model (used GPT-4)
- Fancy UI (table stakes)
- AI "features" (integrated into workflows)

**The moat:**
- Not technology
- Customer-specific embeddings + usage patterns
- Workflow integration depth
- Industry expertise

## Team Building Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Hiring for stage we weren't at**
- Hired enterprise sales exec from Salesforce at $200K
- No repeatable motion, no training, no playbook
- Left after 6 months

**Mistake 2: Over-hiring engineers**
- 12 engineers for 2 customers
- Built things customers didn't ask for
- Perfect code, wrong product

**What worked:**
- Product-minded customer success manager
- Founder who could sell AND deliver
- Small, senior team
- Everyone customer-facing

## The Pricing Evolution

**Pilot phase:** Service model ($50-100K pilots)
**Year 1:** Usage-based ($50-100 per seat/month)
**Year 2:** Outcome-based (percentage of value created)
**Today:** Hybrid (base fee + usage + outcome)

**The learning:** Customers pay for outcomes, not technology. Price to value, not cost.

## The Hardest Moments

**Month 6:** First major customer bug. Lost data. Almost lost account. Worked 72 hours straight to recover.

**Month 14:** Co-founder departure. Culture shock. Thought company would die.

**Month 20:** Competitor raised $50M and started discounting 70%. Had to defend premium positioning.

**Each time:** Thought we were done. Each time, focused on customer success and survived.

## What's Working Now

**Year 3:**
- $8M ARR
- 15 enterprise customers
- 4 products (all in same vertical)
- 35 people

**The playbook:**
1. Deep vertical focus
2. Founder-close for first $2M
3. Land and expand
4. Customer success = growth
5. AI as delivery mechanism, not product

## Key Stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Time to first customer | 8 months |
| Time to $2M ARR | 24 months |
| Current ARR | $8M |
| ACV | $175K |
| Retention | 95% |
| Churn | 0% |
| Initial team size | 5 |
| Current team size | 35 |
| Verticals focused | 1 |

## Advice for Founders Starting Today

1. **Pick a vertical, not a technology**
   - Be "the AI " for one industry
   - Own the entire workflow

2. **Sell service before software**
   - Prove value manually first
   - Then productize

3. **Do things that don't scale**
   - Founder-led sales
   - Founder-led delivery
   - Until playbook is proven

4. **Customer success is sales**
   - Happy customers = new customers
   - Expansion revenue > new logos

5. **Build to outcomes, not features**
   - Customer doesn't want AI
   - Customer wants 40 hours back per week
