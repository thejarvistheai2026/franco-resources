# Planning With Outcome Roadmaps

## Summary

Itamar Gilad presents an alternative to traditional feature roadmaps: outcome roadmaps that focus on goals and learning rather than outputs and timelines.

## The Problem with Classic Roadmaps

- High planning overhead
- High waste from committed features that don't deliver value
- False precision (we don't know what will work)
- Output-focused, not outcome-focused

## The Outcome Roadmap Structure

### Elements

1. **Goals** — Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)
2. **Research** (optional) — Exploratory research to build confidence
3. **Product Discovery** — Validate ideas before building
4. **Product Delivery** — Build validated ideas
5. **Effect Delay** — Time between launch and impact

### Visual Format

Timeline showing:
- Goals as diamonds (completion dates)
- Discovery as exploration phase
- Delivery as execution phase
- Effect delay as lag time

## Key Principles

- **Goals first, features second** — What outcome, not what output
- **Discovery is not optional** — Validate before building
- **Effect delay is real** — Outcomes take time to materialize
- **Adapt as you learn** — Roadmap is directional, not contractual

## Key Insights

- Outcome roadmaps reduce waste by 30-50%
- They require stakeholder education ("where are the features?")
- Best for mature product orgs with discovery muscle
- Can combine with classic roadmaps for hybrid approach
