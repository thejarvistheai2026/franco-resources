#### Details
- He argues there’s a core issue in how many B2B companies run go-to-market (GTM) efforts: they treat channels like cold email, content, social, ads as separate silos instead of a coordinated system.
- Marketing and sales work better when they share goals, insights, and data instead of operating independently.
- Integrated channels tend to perform better than disjointed ones because prospects experience a consistent message at each touchpoint.
- _systems over silos_:
- One thing that ties all of this together: a genuine front-end offer.
	- Across all channels off the same thing
	- Offer something valuable for free — a free audit, a free campaign, a free resource. Hormozi documented this exact model: the business offering free stuff on the front end got 9x more leads at the exact same close rate.
- Use Trigify to monitor your LinkedIn profile 24/7
	- It automatically scrapes everyone who engages with your posts every 24 hours
	- That list gets pushed into Clay
	- Clay runs your ICP qualifier agent — filtering by company size, role, revenue, TAM fit
	- Qualified prospects get routed to cold email, LinkedIn DM, or cold calling
- GTM MAchine:
	- Content → Authority + live intent signals 
	- Intent signals → warm, pre-qualified outbound lists 
	- List building → volume at scale across 5 sources Cold email + LinkedIn + cold calling → consistent meetings 
	- RevOps → closed deals and cash collected
- None of these steps are complicated on their own. What makes it hard is getting all five running simultaneously without any of them breaking down.
