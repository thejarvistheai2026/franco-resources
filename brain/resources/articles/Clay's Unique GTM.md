#### Key Details

6 contrarian moves:

1. Reverse demos killed friction - Product was unusable (8 calls for $200/mo). Let customers drive 30-min screen shares, solved their problem live, got instant UI feedback. Brought sales cycle to zero.

2. Brand before revenue - Bought Clay(.)com, hired claymation artist, made Head of Brand employee #18 pre-revenue. Felt crazy. Became a moat.

3. Usage pricing when everyone said per-seat - First GTM company to do it. Investors said they’re leaving money on table. Actually unlocked technical buyers and land-and-expand motion.

4. Cold email agencies as first customers → UGC flywheel - Agencies became experts, posted on LinkedIn, attracted clients organically. Self-perpetuating cycle. These agencies then became “Claygencies” and has been like an army of forward deployed engineers but not on its payroll.

5. Hired farmers, physicists, archaeologists, magicians - 50% of GTM/G&A doing first job. Prioritized passion/empathy/curiosity over experience. Taught mechanics later.

6. Created GTM Engineering category - Now thousands of GTME jobs exist. Hundreds of agencies built on Clay. First-time founders building 7-figure businesses on top. Community as force multiplier.
