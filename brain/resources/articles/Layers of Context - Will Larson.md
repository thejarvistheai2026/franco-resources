# Layers of Context

## Summary

Will Larson explores how engineering leaders need to operate across multiple layers of context simultaneously — from code-level details to company-wide strategy — and how to navigate between these layers effectively.

## The Layers

1. **Code/Technical** — Implementation details, architecture decisions
2. **Team/Project** — Sprint planning, resource allocation, delivery
3. **Organizational** — Cross-team dependencies, process design
4. **Company** — Strategy, funding, market position
5. **Industry** — Competitive landscape, technology trends

## Key Insights

- Effective leaders context-switch between layers constantly
- Different stakeholders need information at different layers
- The "right" layer depends on the decision being made
- Over-indexing on any single layer creates blind spots

### Communication Across Layers

- **Upward communication** — Translate technical to business impact
- **Downward communication** — Translate strategy to concrete actions
- **Peer communication** — Shared context, nuanced trade-offs

## Key Quotes

> "The best engineering leaders are comfortable moving between layers of abstraction, never losing sight of the details while keeping the bigger picture in mind."
