#### Key Details
- A playbook for identifying what to automate, building it, proving its value, and becoming the person your company can't afford to lose.
- The Audit
	- Open a blank doc. Write down every recurring task you did this past week. Not the big projects. The stuff. The formatting, the data pulls, the emails that follow a pattern, the reports nobody reads but everybody expects.
	- Now star anything that hits all three:
		- You do it the same way every time
		- It takes more than 30 minutes
		- Someone else sees the output
- The First Build
	- Open Claude (or ChatGPT). Take the task you just picked and type this:
		- I need to automate a recurring task. Here's how I currently do it, step by step:
			- Paste your steps here. Be specific. "Open HubSpot, filter deals by close date, export to CSV, paste into Google Sheets template, write 3-sentence summary for my manager" 
			- Which steps are mechanical (a machine could do them identically every time) and which require my judgment? Then suggest the simplest automation I could build this week using Zapier, Make, or just a reusable Claude prompt.
- 2 ways AI is used at work:
	- The Coaster: You quietly use ChatGPT to finish your work in 2 hours, then scroll Reddit for 6. This works until someone notices your output is suspiciously uniform, your growth has flatlined, and your entire job can be done by a prompt. You've proven you're replaceable.
	- The Ostrich: You figure your expertise protects you. Meanwhile, someone on a competing team does your 4-hour report in 20 minutes and spends the extra time on strategic projects. They get promoted. You get "managed out."
	- What actually works: Use AI to kill the tedious 80% of your job so you can pour your energy into the 20% that requires a human brain—then make damn sure everyone knows you're doing it.

- The goal isn't to work less. It's to reallocate your time from low-value tasks to high-value work, and to become the person who helps your entire team do the same.

Here's the cycle. It's not a checklist. It compounds.
1. Audit. Find the tasks eating your time.
2. Evaluate. Pick the right one to automate first.
3. Build. Create the automation (usually simpler than you think).
4. Validate. Make sure it actually works.
5. Socialize. Show the win to your team and leadership.
6. Repeat. Move to the next task, with more credibility each time.
