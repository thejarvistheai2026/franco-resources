# How Figma Grows

## Summary

Deep dive into Figma's product-led growth strategy, from stealth mode through $20B valuation, analyzing their community-led growth, product strategy, and competitive positioning.

## Key Insights

### The Origin Story

- Dylan Field won Thiel Fellowship (dropped out of Brown)
- Co-founder Evan Wallace was CS TA who built WebGL demos
- Built for 3 years before launch (2012-2015)
- First customer: Coda (used full-time before public launch)

### Product-Led Growth Motion

1. **Community-first** — Design community evangelized before enterprise sales
2. **Collaboration as distribution** — Every file shared = viral loop
3. **Freemium with team upsell** — Individual → Team → Organization
4. **Plugin ecosystem** — Third-party developers extended value

### Why Figma Won

- **Real-time collaboration** — Google Docs for design
- **Web-first** — No installation, works everywhere
- **Design systems** — Shared components across teams
- **Developer handoff** — Bridged design/engineering gap

### Competitive Dynamics

- Sketch had market share but no collaboration
- Adobe XD was late and half-hearted
- Figma's web-first was initially seen as limitation, became advantage

## Key Lessons

- 3 years of stealth allowed product-market fit before hype
- Community-led growth beats sales-led for design tools
- Collaboration features create natural viral loops
- Platform strategy (plugins) compounds value over time
