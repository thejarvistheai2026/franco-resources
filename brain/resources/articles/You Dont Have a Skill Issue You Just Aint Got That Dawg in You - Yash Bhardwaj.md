# You Don't Have a Skill Issue, You Just Ain't Got That Dawg in You

## Summary

Yash Bhardwaj argues that the world has an abundance of skills but an extreme scarcity of courage. Success comes not from hoarding courses and information, but from having the courage to act despite imperfection.

## The Core Argument

**The world's most acceptable excuse:** "I can't do this because I lack the skills"

This reasoning removes accountability and creates complacency. You keep hoarding courses but the goalpost keeps shifting.

**The reality:** The most skilled people aren't always the wealthiest. The "cringe" creators with courage outperform the skilled perfectionists with unreleased work.

## Three Ways to Imbibe This Mindset

### 1. Seek Forgiveness, Not Permission

If you're building something disruptive, the world doesn't want to be disrupted.
- Investors won't get your idea
- Peers will think you're insane
- If you wait for permission, the answer is likely no

**The shift:** Act first, let the world react to you. The only permission you need is your own.

### 2. What Would You Do If You Knew You Couldn't Fail?

Don't start from limitations — begin with possibilities.

**Dream exercise:**
- What would you do if you knew 100% you couldn't fail?
- Assume infinite money + resources
- Don't write about the how, just dream

### 3. Ship in Prod

**The problem:** You became careful. In measuring consequences, you forgot the joy of making mistakes.

**The solution:** Give yourself permission to suck initially, then improve.

> "Action produces information. Get the fuck out of localhost."

## Key Quote

> "Life shrinks or expands according to one's courage." — Anais Nin
