## Summary

Mark Vassilevskiy argues that in an era where technology is cheap and easy (OpenClaw, Claude Code, Perplexity), distribution has become the primary differentiator for startup success. The tech/distribution ratio has shifted from 90/10 to potentially 1/99, making distribution 75-80% of success.

## Key Thesis/Insights

- **Tech is no longer the barrier**: Anyone with a laptop can build a platform overnight using AI tools
- **Distribution is not followers/views**: It's consistency of spreading the right message to the right people
- **Three types of distribution**: UGC, Paid Marketing, Organic Content
- **The future ratio**: As tech becomes trivial (possibly via Neuralink), distribution becomes 99% of success
- **Distribution creates moats**: With distribution, you're not risking getting replaced by competition

## Main Content Breakdown

### The Shift in Tech/Distribution Ratio

**Historical progression:**
- 90/10 (tech mattered most) → 80/20 → 60/40 → 50/50 → 70/30 → **75-80% distribution now**
- Future: 1/99 (think of idea → working MVP in seconds)

### What Distribution Is NOT

- Not followers
- Not views/volume
- Not marketing
- Not millions of views on ragebait

**What it IS**: Consistency of spreading the right message across the right people (ICP)

### Three Types of Distribution

1. **UGC (User Generated Content)**
   - Pay creators/users to talk about your product
   - People hear about it from every corner
   - See actual users using it

2. **Paid Marketing**
   - Most predictable, most cost-inefficient
   - Find winning CPM, repeat with more budget
   - Cash intensive, attention getting more expensive

3. **Organic Content** (Mark's favorite)
   - **Everyday content**: Never-ending loop of creation
   - **Big launches**: Spike of attention (e.g., Perplexity Computer video → 20M+ views in 24h)
   - Best strategy: Combination of both

### Examples of Good Distribution

- **AnthropicAI**: New launch 1-2x per week, consistent global conversation
- **Superhuman, Granola, Ramp**: Great distribution examples
- **@levelsio**: Micro-SaaS wins via distribution, not tech complexity
- **@jackfriks/PostBridge**: Survives vs Typefully/Hootsuite via distribution
- **@zach_yadegari/Cal AI**: Sold calorie-counting app (basically what ChatGPT can do) via right distribution

### SpaceX Example

**Why SpaceX IPO at $1.5T valuation?**
- Elon is "king of distribution"
- Tesla market cap (~$1.2T) > all other car companies combined
- Not about revenue/sales — about distribution power
- Valuation depends on Elon's ability to inspire
- Blue Origin/Chinese companies can match tech, never the capitalization

### Naval's Point

> "Pure software is rapidly becoming un-investable."

Tech alone isn't profitable anymore.

### The Strategy

**Make something for select few, but what the whole world discusses:**
- Sell $500k landing page to small group → everyone talks about it
- Sydney Sweeney bathwater → couple hundred buyers, weeks of news
- Use attention as funnel for bigger product

how to win in an era where the tech is cheap and distribution is everything

it has never been easier to build a product OpenClaw. Claude Code. Perplexity Computer. new tools come out every day and kill a bunch of smaller startups but how not to get killed overnight? the answer is - Distribution

throughout the whole history, creating a startup has always been a balance of tech & distribution back in the days to build a product, you had to hire 5 devs, 2 designers, a project manager and spend a month doing the first MVP now it's just one person with Claude Code. the barrier to enter and start to execute became much lower. anyone with a laptop can build a platform overnight. tech isn't an issue anymore (for the most part, as openclaw can't build a rocket for u yet)

tech/distribution ratio is changing rapidly and went from 90/10 (when the tech mattered the most) to 80/20, 60/40, 50/50, 70/30... now I think distribution is probably 75-80% of the success

in the future, you'll be able to just think about an idea and get the working MVP in front of your eyes within a second. maybe it's gonna be in the faraway future when all of us will use Neuralink, but we're going in this direction anyway. in this scenario, the ratio is gonna be 1/99

however, distribution isn't followers. it's not how many views you get. It's not volume. and it's not marketing it does correlate with these things, but they don't define it getting millions of views/followers in front of the wrong audience won't help your product. getting millions of views on ragebaits also won't help you much. you can literally start posting memes tmrw and get thousands of likes, but the chance that these likes will convert into paying users is close to zero

so what is it? Distribution is the consistency of spreading the right message across the right people. It's how you can show your brand in a positive way in front of the people who are actually gonna buy/use it

Distribution is the reason why the attention economy exists. people buy expensive domains, spend hundreds of thousands of dollars on Launch Videos & UGC - just to spread their message across the whole world but the key here is to be consistent, as startups pump & dump really quick, yesterday everyone was talking about the purchase of ai.com and now everyone has forgotten about them. ClubHouse, Apple Vision Pro, Cluely... you name it.

so, now that we understand what distribution is, let's talk about how to actually leverage that

**3 types of distribution**

throughout my career, i've found 3 most common and effective ways of distribution:
- UGC
- Paid Marketing
- Organic Content

each method has its cons & pros and lemme quickly go all over them

UGC - you pay other creators/users to talk about your product and create content around it. as a result, people don't have to follow your page to get to know about you but instead they hear about it from every corner and see how actual users use it. again, distribution isn't how many followers or views you can have on your company profile, it's a total amount of people (preferrably ICP) heard about your product.

Paid Marketing - the most predictable and the most cost uneffecient way of capturing attention. it's predictable cuz you do a couple of campaigns with smaller budget, find the perfect CPM and then repeat the same winning campaign over and over again by putting more and more money into it and creating all sorts of funnels. but at the same time it's not something you can start with $0 budget, it's cash intensive and with every day capturing attention becomes more and more expensive.

Organic Content - my favorite way of distributing and something that I believe I mastered pretty well. I'd divide it into 2 parts as well: everyday content & big launches. in the first one, you just create content, people get to know about you, you create more content, it's a never ending loop. in the second option you create big launches (aka launch videos) and get a pretty big spike of attention & retention overnight. everybody knows about Perplexity but the moment they release Perplexity Computer, their video gets 20m+ views within 24h. a good organic content strategy for every startup is a combination of both of these parts.

**What are the examples of good distribution?**

let's see a couple of examples of this "distribution" in today's world and atm I think @AnthropicAI might be the king here. they make a new launch once or twice per week and make people around the whole world talk about them consistently

startups like @Superhuman, @meetgranola, @tryramp are also great examples

@levelsio has a bunch of micro-SaaS that solve some specific problems, but are not very difficult to replicate in terms of tech. he wins cuz of great distribution

@jackfriks is also a great example. he built PostBridge, a post-scheduling tool. but how could PostBridge survive when there are companies like Typefully and Hootsuite on the market? and again, the answer is distribution

and I guess most of you heard the news that @zach_yadegari sold Cal AI. an app that just counts your calories by taking a picture of the food, basically what ChatGPT can do, but because of the right distribution, he was able to capture the size of the market

lowkey you can take a look at the most startups featured on @starter_story and see that most of them are successful because of the distribution

take a look at the recent Naval's post that tech. just investing in tech isn't profitable anymore

Naval @naval Mar 1: "Pure software is rapidly becoming un-investable."

**let's talk about SpaceX**

I've had this discussion with a friend of mine a couple of weeks ago I was curious, why would SpaceX announce its $1.5 trillion IPO when they already have enough cash and future contracts and it's all because @elonmusk himself is a king of distribution.

Tesla market cap is around ~$1.2 trilion, it's basically bigger than all other car companies in the world combined but why is Tesla valued so much when Toyota is making more revenue, making more sales, and has existed much longer on the market? the question is the distribution behind Elon and why he's a great leader. he can convince all people around the world that Tesla is the future of the world. the price does not depend on sales reports anymore, it depends solely on his figure

that's why having an IPO for SpaceX is basically getting free money for him. Blue Origin or some Chinese companies can get closer to SpaceX in terms of tech, but they'll never reach the same capitalization level, as people (including me) will also get inspired by SpaceX and will see the future of humanity in this company. having a company on IPO for Elon is just necessary. the valuation is completely dependent on his distribution power

**One last thing**

make smth for select a few, but what the whole world is gonna discuss

you can sell a $500k landing page for a small group of people, but everyone is gonna talk about it

Sydney Sweeney can sell her bathwater to just a couple of hundred people around the world, but she's gonna be in the news for the next couple of weeks

you can always use this attention as a funnel for a bigger product, or just sell something extremely expensive, which is gonna drive you a lot of revenue anyway

in a nutshell, distribution is the only thing in today's world that can help you to stay unique among millions of startups that launch on a daily basis

with distribution, you're not risking getting replaced. you're not risking a competition. you're just consistently searching for more ways to get people's attention as it becomes the new currency
