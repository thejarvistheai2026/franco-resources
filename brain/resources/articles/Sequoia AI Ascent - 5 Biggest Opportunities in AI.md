# Sequoia AI Ascent: The 5 Biggest Opportunities in AI

## Summary

Alfred Lin (Sequoia Capital) shares the 5 biggest opportunities in AI from Sequoia's AI Ascent event. These represent the areas where Sequoia sees the most significant value creation potential in the current AI landscape.

## The 5 Biggest Opportunities

### 1. AI Infrastructure

**The opportunity:** Building the picks and shovels for the AI gold rush.

**Key areas:**
- Compute infrastructure (GPUs, TPUs, custom silicon)
- Model serving and inference optimization
- Data infrastructure for AI workloads
- Developer tools and MLOps

**Why it matters:** Every AI application needs infrastructure. As AI scales, infrastructure needs compound.

### 2. Vertical AI Applications

**The opportunity:** AI-native applications for specific industries.

**Key insight:** Horizontal AI (ChatGPT, Claude) is becoming commoditized. The value is moving to vertical solutions with deep domain expertise.

**Examples:**
- Legal (Harvey, Evenup)
- Healthcare (Ambience, Suki)
- Finance (accounting, underwriting)
- Manufacturing
- Education

**The moat:** Proprietary data + industry-specific workflows + compliance expertise.

### 3. AI Agents

**The opportunity:** Autonomous agents that can perform complex tasks.

**The shift:** From copilots (assistants) to agents (autonomous workers).

**Key capabilities:**
- Planning and reasoning
- Tool use
- Memory and state management
- Multi-step task execution

**Use cases:**
- Software engineering
- Customer service
- Research and analysis
- Sales and marketing

### 4. Consumer AI

**The opportunity:** AI-native consumer applications.

**The insight:** We're in the "Cambrian explosion" of consumer AI. New categories are being created.

**Categories:**
- AI companions
- Content creation
- Education and learning
- Health and wellness
- Creativity and entertainment

**Key question:** What new behaviors will AI enable that weren't possible before?

### 5. Enterprise AI

**The opportunity:** AI transformation of enterprise workflows.

**The shift:** From "AI features" to "AI-native workflows."

**Key areas:**
- Knowledge management
- Process automation
- Decision support
- Customer-facing AI
- Internal tools

**The challenge:** Enterprise sales cycles, security requirements, integration complexity.

**The reward:** Massive contract values, high switching costs, deep moats.

## Key Themes Across All 5

1. **Infrastructure first** — The foundation must be built before applications can thrive
2. **Vertical over horizontal** — Deep expertise beats generalist tools
3. **Agents are the new apps** — Autonomy changes what's possible
4. **Consumer is wide open** — New categories being created
5. **Enterprise is hard but valuable** — Long cycles, big rewards

## The Sequoia Perspective

**What they're looking for:**
- Founders with deep technical expertise
- Clear understanding of the problem space
- Sustainable moats (data, workflow, distribution)
- Willingness to build for the long term

**What they're avoiding:**
- Thin wrappers around GPT-4
- Solutions looking for problems
- Commoditized horizontal tools
- Short-term thinking

## Key Quote

> "The biggest opportunities in AI aren't in building the models. They're in applying AI to transform industries, workflows, and human capabilities."
