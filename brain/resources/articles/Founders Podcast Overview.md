#### Key lessons

- **Mission + Simple Interface:** A life needs a mission & make it clear & simple to others. Steve Jobs: “Insanely great products”
- **Hustle + Obsession** - a cult/religious obsession with the mission that drives work more, longer, think about it over and over, iterations….
- **Learn + Student of the Craft** - study the great work that came before you. there is always a playbook, don’t repeat same mistakes… get an Information edge by collecting, out learning others….
- **Opportunity/Niche in plain sigh**t - what have others rejected? what *“treasure map”* can you build? what do you have that others don’t?
- **Business is problems** - they are unavoidable, but therein lie the opportunities. problems are just opportunities and workflows. Don’t run, embrace them as the process. If you have no problems… there is a big problem.
- **Simplicity in Complexity** - knowing your business from A-Z in order to have simplicity emerge.
- **Multi-Vector Attack** - have a 360 view of the problem and launch a relentless, multiprogned attack.
- **The Paid Critic** - speaking not as a critic, but as the ultimate advocate.
- **Do the best job** - do the best you can with the opportunity in front of you. Opportunity handled well leads to more opportunity.
- **Tell The Story** - explain to your customers what goes into your product. It may be bland to you, but it will increase its value to them…
- **Key questions to have a successful company** - why are we doing this?
- Quality is never an accident. It is always the result of high intention, sincere effort, intelligent direction, and skillful execution. It represents the wise choice of many alternatives.
- 2 things written on my desk:
    - Do one thing relentlessly.
    - What assets do I have that I’m not using?
- if you wanted to be truly differentiated, what would you do?

- do everything and you will win.
- It’s not what you do but how you do it
- create the best customer experience and work backwards from that.
- you have to stay in the game long enough to get lucky.
- Relationships run the world.
- history does not repeat, human nature does.
- being detached from the customer is the ultimate death.
- We are rationalizing creatures. We are not rational ones.
- Think before you act and then burn the boats.
- The important things of tomorrow are probably going to be things that are overlooked today.

- Companies, like individuals, do not become exceptional by believing they are exceptional, but by understanding the ways in which they aren't exceptional.
- Be curious, relentlessly curious.
- Business, like life, is all about how you make people feel. It's that simple and that hard.
- the hard work necessary to build the business one element/customer at a time.
- The road to success is paved with mistakes, well handled.
- Speed matters.
