#### Summary
A breakdown of what each price tier ($500–$200K+) actually requires to deliver AI & automation services — not just what companies pay, but the processes, team structures, and timelines needed on the delivery side.

#### Core Insight

| Tier                                  | Price       | Time to Deliver | Team Required            | What You Deliver                                                            |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------- | --------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **AI Tool Setup**                     | $500–$2K    | 2–5 hours       | Just you                 | Claude/GPT workflows, custom instructions, basic automation + team training |
| **Full Audit + Strategy**             | $500–$5K    | 10–14 days      | Just you                 | Process map, bottleneck report, ROI calc, implementation roadmap            |
| **Single Workflow Automation**        | $2K–$5K     | 1–2 weeks       | You or 1 dev             | One end-to-end workflow (intake, routing, proposals, onboarding, etc.)      |
| **Full Department Build**             | $8K–$25K    | 4–8 weeks       | You + 1–2 devs           | Entire department OS (sales, ops, fulfillment, or support)                  |
| **AI-Native Operating System**        | $25K–$60K   | 2–3 months      | You + 3–4 devs           | Company Brain — connects all departments, centralizes data                  |
| **Enterprise Multi-Dept + AI Agents** | $60K–$200K+ | 3–6 months      | Full bench (4+ devs, PM) | Org-wide system with dedicated AI agents per function                       |

- **Communication is the $500 skill that pays off at $60K**  
	- Learning to explain AI to non-technical people becomes worth a fortune later.

- **The $2K strategy audit is the most important tier**  
	- Everything bigger depends on it. It's also where you learn if the client is actually worth building for.

- **Simplicity wins**  
	- "The biggest results I've delivered came from the simplest builds."

- **Clients pay for clarity, not complexity**  
	- "You're not selling automation anymore. You're selling operational transformation."

- **The headcount replacement model**  
	- If your system replaces $300K in annual labor, charging $80K is easy math for a CFO.

## The Build Cycle (Department Tier)

1. **Foundations:** tables, records, relationships, permissions
2. **Workflow automations:** intake, routing, approvals, notifications
3. **Intelligence layer:** AI agents for data processing, recommendations
4. **QA:** test failure modes, bad inputs, edge cases, duplicates
5. **Handoff:** walkthrough, training videos, documentation, support plan

## Why This Matters

> "Don't try to jump from $2K to $60K overnight. Every tier teaches you something the next one requires."

The ladder is real — but you have to climb it. Each rung makes the next one possible.
