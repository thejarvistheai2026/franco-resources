# How SaaS Companies Can Be Saved

**Source:** Eoghan McCabe (@eoghan), CEO of Intercom  
**URL:** https://x.com/eoghan/status/2028522852044206258

## Summary

SaaS companies must practice "creative destruction" — actively killing their old workflow-based business to survive the AI agent transition. Intercom serves as a case study: through aggressive pivoting toward AI agents (their product "Fin"), they reversed from negative growth to becoming potentially the fastest-growing large software company in the world, now at $400M ARR.

## Key Points

- **The market has shifted:** AI agents commodify code and render traditional workflow tools less important
- **Intercom's turnaround:** From negative growth to $400M ARR (Fin approaching $100M), doubling growth rate two years straight while peers declined
- **The hard truth:** Agents don't need workflow tools — if you don't cannibalize your own CRUD app business, someone else will
- **"Destroy your past" tactics Intercom used:**
  - Rewrote company values and mission around AI agents
  - Shifted 80% R&D focus to Fin (while it was <10% of revenue)
  - Bought $1M .ai domain, created separate brand/site
  - Killed ~$60M ARR with new outcome-based pricing model
  - Grew AI team 10x (6→60 people) with PhD-level researchers
  - Built their own foundational models
  - Changed board composition (removed "mature" leaders for startup founders)
  - Focused marketing entirely on Fin, not Intercom

## Notes

**Why they could do it:**
- Desperate for growth (performing far worse than average = less to lose)
- CEO change made it easier to rip up old plans
- Right place/time: already building AI bots for years, obvious that service software would be decimated by agents

**The tough pill for SaaS leaders:** "All it will take is destroying everything you love."

**On timing:** Not too late for latecomers — customer adoption remains slow in many sectors despite fast-moving tech.
