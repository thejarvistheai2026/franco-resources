# Writers Who Operate

## Summary

Will Larson discusses the intersection of writing and operations — how the best operational leaders are also skilled writers, and why writing is a core competency for effective execution.

## The Thesis

Writing is not just communication — it's thinking. Leaders who write well:
- Think more clearly
- Align teams more effectively
- Create durable organizational knowledge

## Writing as Operational Tool

### Types of Operational Writing

1. **Strategy documents** — Align on direction
2. **Decision records** — Capture rationale
3. **Playbooks** — Codify processes
4. **Retrospectives** — Learn from outcomes
5. **RFCs** — Propose changes

### Writing Quality Indicators

- Clear structure (problem, solution, trade-offs)
- Specific examples over abstractions
- Acknowledged constraints and assumptions
- Clear ownership and next steps

## Key Insights

- Good writing saves meeting time
- Written artifacts compound over time
- Writing forces clarity that speaking doesn't
- The best operators are prolific writers

## Key Quotes

> "If you can't write it down, you haven't thought it through."
