# Why 90% of Founders Fail at Organic Marketing

## Summary

After generating billions of organic views in 3 years without spending on ads, jorgia breaks down why most founders fail at organic marketing. It's not that organic doesn't work — it's that founders fundamentally misunderstand how social media actually works. The thread outlines 5 critical mistakes and a framework for treating content as an engine, not random creativity.

## The Core Problem

> "Most founders aren't failing because organic marketing is hard, you're failing because you fundamentally misunderstand how social media actually works."

**What founders do:**
- Post a few random videos
- Copy viral trends
- Talk about features
- Give up after 15 posts

**OR**
- Come up with what YOU think is good
- Use automated tools to schedule/mass post
- Have Claude bot do it for you

**Then conclude:** "Organic doesn't work" or "organic is dead"

**Reality:** Organic absolutely works. But you need to understand the mechanics.

## The 5 Critical Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Treating Social Media Like "Traditional Ads"

**Founder approach:** "Let me show people my product!"

**Why it dies:** Social media is not advertising. It is **algorithmic entertainment**.

**What platforms care about:** Retention. Period.

| If Content... | Then... |
|---------------|---------|
| Holds attention | Algorithm distributes it |
| Doesn't hold attention | Dies instantly |

**First rule:** Your content must be valuable even if someone never downloads your app.

> "If the only value in your content is your product, it will fail."

### Mistake 2: Starting With the Product

**Founder approach:** "We built an app that helps you…"

**Why it fails:** Nobody cares. Not because your product isn't good — because the viewer hasn't been given a reason to care yet.

**Attention works in stages:**

1. **Hook/pattern interruption** → stop the scroll
2. **Curiosity** → make them stay
3. **Emotion** → make them care (that you can solve THEIR problem)
4. **Product** → introduce the solution

**The error:** Founders skip 1-3 and jump straight to 4.

### Mistake 3: Posting Without a Format

**Reality:** Organic marketing is not random creativity. It's **repeatable formats**.

**The playbook:**
- Find a format that works
- Run it again and again until it dries up
- Then run it again with different hooks

> "Why fix something that ain't broke"... EXACTLY!

**High-converting content categories:**
- UGC videos
- Reaction videos
- AI videos
- Slideshows

**Top 2 converters:**

**1. Reaction + Demo**
- Someone reacts to a problem
- Then your app solves it
- **Why it works:** Reaction creates emotional context

**2. "POV" content**
- Viewers see themselves in the content
- Recognize their own pain point, emotional trigger, interest
- Watch to see what you're suggesting will solve it
- **Why it works:** Directly poking their emotion and aggravating it

### Mistake 4: Not Posting Enough

**Founder mistake:** Post 10 pieces of content and expect results.

**Reality:** Organic distribution is a **volume game with feedback loops**.

**Your first 50–100 posts are not marketing. They are market research.**

Each post teaches you:
- Which hooks work
- Which emotions trigger comments
- Which formats hold attention

**Platform sensitivity (TikTok/Instagram):**
- Using a VPN?
- Did it drop out?
- Platform outage?
- Mentioned a word they don't like?
- Spam posts too close together?
- Edited bio too quickly?

> "You're basically signing onto a toxic relationship."

**The growth pattern:**
- Once something works, scale that format
- Repeat. And repeat. And repeat until it's dead
- **Only happens with pure volume** (don't spam posts though)

### Mistake 5: Thinking Views = Success

![Views vs Conversions](why-90-percent-founders-fail-organic-marketing.jpg)

**The trap:** Views feel good. Checking dashboards and view counts are some of the biggest dopamine hits you'll ever feel.

**The real question:** Did the content create emotional charge and curiosity about the product?

**Great content does three things:**
1. Stops the scroll
2. Creates emotional engagement
3. Makes the viewer curious about the product

**When all three happen:** You've initiated the "golden flow."

**The app founder disadvantage:**

| Amazon Brand / TikTok Shop | App Founder |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| "Linked in bio" — direct, quick, in-platform journey | Must ask viewer to: leave platform → open App Store → search → find → download → onboard |
| Can get away with less emotional spark | Must edge them right from hook or they never finish |

> "Views are hot. Conversions are hotter."

## The Reality of Organic Distribution

Organic growth is not magic. It's a **system**.

**What winning founders do:**
- Treat content like an engine
- Test formats
- Study retention
- Iterate relentlessly
- Once they find something that works, scale it aggressively

**The advantage:** Eliminates need to "piss away a boat load of money on paid ads that viewers probably won't care about."

**The workflow:**
1. Spend 5 mins making/posting a single TikTok
2. See how audience relates to it
3. Use that data to propel forward
4. Once something works organically → move it into paid distribution

> "Could you imagine a founder burning BUNDLES of cash on paid distribution only for it to totally flop because they didn't do a FREE pre-test? This is probably you until right now..."

## The Opportunity Right Now

**The shift:** AI made building apps the easiest thing in the world.

**The result:** **Distribution became the new moat.**

**The unfair advantage:** Founders who TRULY understand organic distribution — not from echo chambers of "shadowbans," "VPNs," "auto-posting" on X, but from someone boots on the ground with years of ACTUAL results and manual labour.

**Why it matters:**
- Not because their apps are better
- Because they understand how to capture attention

> "In today's world: Attention is the real distribution channel. Attention is today's currency."
