# Community-led growth is the ultimate moat for AI startups

**Author:** Annie Liao (@AnnieLiao_2000)  
**Source:** [X/Twitter](https://x.com/AnnieLiao_2000/status/2028693049598157058)  
**Context:** Built 50K+ AI builder community across 60+ cities via IRL events. Partners include Manus (Academy), Lovable (Shipped), and Gemini Studio.

## The Core Thesis

In an attention economy, community cuts through noise by pulling on the innate human desire for connection. When someone builds with your tool in a room full of peers, switching costs change. The narrative shifts from "I tried it" to "I'm part of this."

---

## The Three Flywheels

### 1. PLG Flywheel (Product-Led Growth)
```
Marketing → First IRL Event → Engaged Member → PLG Lead Gen
```
Your best customers don't just buy—they advocate. Community compresses the distance between awareness and revenue.

### 2. SLG Flywheel (Sales-Led Growth)
```
City Lead → Ambassador → Affiliate for Enterprise Product → Revenue
```
When ambassadors refer enterprise deals and partnerships, engagement becomes revenue. Launch this when the sales playbook becomes repeatable.

### 3. Partnerships Flywheel
Growth hack by onboarding partners/collaborators into your ecosystem via IRL activations, ambassador networks, academy infrastructure, and enterprise enablement. This creates an asymmetric advantage and a difficult-to-replicate revenue loop.

---

## The Exact Playbook

### 1. Start With a Super Specific ICP
Example: Build Club started with technical AI engineers and startup founders only. Narrow focus reveals signal (e.g., learners outnumber builders → new academy products for technically lite audiences).

### 2. Find Your One Format — Then Own It
Iterate on format before scaling. For Build Club: co-working with strong focus on building.
- Repeatable
- High-trust
- Operator-friendly
- Upgrade deliberately: good lighting, playlists, minimal intentional branding

### 3. Don't Recruit City Leads. Attract Them.
Flywheel: Great Events → Engaged Members → Inbound City Leads → More Great Events
- Give operators a tight playbook
- Clear ownership
- Direct access to core team
- Treat them as partners, not contractors

### 4. Incentive Alignment Is the Engine
Onboard City Leads 1:1 with:
- Community manifesto
- Branding playbooks
- Affiliate structures
- Enterprise referral pathways
- Partnership revenue splits

**Key concept:** The "model citizen" — when a community has a clear, relatable archetype to aspire to, it grounds the vision/mission in truth.

### 5. Your Tech Stack Should Embarrass You
Build Club still uses Airtable, Notion, Google Docs. Uses Manus AI to manage and surface Slack messages (built in one hour).

> "If you're investing in $$ community platforms (Circle, Skilljar, Skool) before validating operator density... you're procrastinating."

**Critical:** Community must plug directly into partnerships, product, and revenue. If it lives in isolation, it dies.

## Personnel: Don't Hire a Community Manager

**Hire a Platform Manager instead.**

Wrong: Event planner  
Right: Systems operator who:
- Thinks in systems
- Is an extrovert with creative flair
- Has magnetism and strong bias to action
- Acts as a distribution architect
- Sits at intersection of community, partnerships, product, and sales

---

## The North Star: Institutions as Community Models

Studying historically successful communities (churches, scouts):
- Decentralized (or symbolically anchored to one leader)
- Structured around playbooks and incentive systems
- Locally autonomous, centrally aligned
- Scale because of structure, belonging, and shared mission
