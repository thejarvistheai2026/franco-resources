#### Key Details
- "One cannot build performance on weaknesses, let alone on something one cannot do at all." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 2

- "Whenever you make a key decision or take a key action, write down what you expect will happen. None or 12 months later, compare the actual results with your expectations." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 3

- "First and foremost, concentrate on your strengths. Put yourself where your strengths can produce results. Second, work on improving your strengths. Third, discover where your intellectual arrogance is causing disabling ignorance and overcome it." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 6

- ^^"It is equally essential to remedy your bad habits-the things you do or fail to do that inhibit your effectiveness and performance."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 7

- "We all have a vast number of areas in which we have no talent or skill and little chance of becoming even mediocre. In those areas person and especially a knowledge worker-should not take on work, jobs, and assignments. One should waste as little effort as possible on improving areas of low competence." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 9

- ^^"Energy. resources, and time should go instead to making a competent person into a star performer."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 10

- ^^"For knowledge workers, How do I perform? may be an even more important question than What are my strengths?"^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 10

- "Like one's strengths, how one performs is unique. It is a matter of personality." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 11

- "Just as people achieve results by doing what they are good at, they also achieve results by working in ways that they best perform." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 11

- ^^"The first thing to know is whether you are a reader or a listener."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 11

- "The second thing to know about how one performs is to know how one learns." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 15

- "The explanation is that writers do not, as a rule, learn by listening and reading. They learn by writing." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 16

- "Do I work well with people, or am I a loner? And if you do work well with people, you must ask, in what relationship?" ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 19

- "Another crucial question is, ^^Do I produce results as a decision maker or as an adviser?^^ A great many people perform best as advisers but cannot take the burden and pressure of making the decision. A good many other people, by contrast, need an adviser to force themselves to think; then they can make decisions and act on them with speed, self confidence, and courage. The top spot requires a decision maker." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 20

- "Do I perform well under stress. or do I need a highly structured and predictable environment? Do I work best in a big organization or a small one? Few people work well in all kinds of environments." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 21

- ^^"The conclusion bears repeating: Do not try to change yourself you are unlikely to succeed. But work hard to improve the way you perform."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 22

- "To be able to manage yourself, you finally have to ask, ^^What are my values?^^ This is not a question of ethics." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 22

- ^^"That is the mirror test. Ethics requires that you ask yourself, What kind of person do l want to see in the mirror in the morning?"^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 23

- "To work in an organization whose value system is unacceptable or incompatible with one's own condemns a person both to frustration and to nonperformance." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 24

- "A person's strengths and the way that person performs rarely conflict; the two are complementary." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 29

- "^^Successful careers are not planned. They develop when people are prepared for opportunities because they know their strengths, their method of work, and their values."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 32

- "What should my contribution be? To answer it, they must address three distinct elements: ^^What does the situation require? Given my strengths, my way of performing, and my values, how can I make the greatest contribution to what needs to be done? And finally, What results have to be achieved to make a difference?"^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 35

- "Where and how can I achieve results that will make a difference within the next year and a half'? ^^ First, the results should be hard to achieve [...] but also, they should be within reach. Second, the results should be meaningful. They should make a difference. Finally, results should be visible and, if at all possible, measurable. ^^ " ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 37

- "It is incumbent on the people who work with them to observe them, to find out how they work, and to adapt themselves to what makes their bosses most effective. This, in fact, is the secret of " managing" the boss. The same holds true for all your coworkers. Each works his or her way, not your way." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 40

- ^^"The first secret of effectiveness is to understand the people you work with and depend on so that you can make use of their strengths, their ways of working, and their values."^^ ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 40

- "There is one prerequisite for managing the second half of your life: You must begin long before you enter it." ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 50

- "^^In effect, managing oneself demands that each knowledge worker think and behave like a chief executive officer.^^" ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 55

- "But today the opposite is true. **Knowledge workers outlive organizations, and they are mobile. The need to manage oneself is therefore creating a revolution in human affairs."** ― Peter Ferdinand Drucker, Managing Oneself, p. 55 !
