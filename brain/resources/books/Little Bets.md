#### Key details
- Why put everything you’ve got into huge, expensive undertakings that cost lots of time and money?
- Little bets add up over time and even develop into complete businesses
- Rather than starting out with the BIG idea, entrepreneurs usually discover it while they are working on smaller concepts.
- A little bet has the advantage of being cheap, quick, and of letting you work your way through uncertainty
- Small steps are low-cost and can be launched speedily, companies won’t collapse if one bet fails
- Even if they go wrong, they allow us to fail swiftly and learn from the outcome
- What really makes great entrepreneurs is their ability to abandon ideas that may have once seemed great, rather than just sticking to a single vision
- Growth-mindset enables you to do more than with a fixed-mindset
- A prototype is an example of “healthy- perfectionism” - it’s fast, cheap, and therefore a small bet that enables you to learn
- You can actually use constraints to limit the breadth of your project and pinpoint problems that need to be tackled
- “Smallifying”: self-impose constraints to break down a larger project into more manageable steps that can be easily attained
- “Worm’s eye view” vs “bird’s eye view”
- The Medici effect (bringing together all types of people to learn from)
- Leverage small wins to track progress - but also to act as sign posts for new directions
