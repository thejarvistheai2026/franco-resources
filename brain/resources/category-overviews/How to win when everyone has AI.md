#### Key Details

- For roughly 50 years, real economic progress has underperformed historical norms. We improved interfaces and software, but the core economy barely moved. Productivity growth fell well below postwar levels. At the same time, birth rates continue to decline. The math stopped working.
	- [[[CRM] Marc Andreesseen]]

- The second mistake is assuming AI flattens everyone. It does the opposite. AI magnifies differences.

- This brings us to the most important individual strategy in an AI-driven economy: The talent stack.

- **There are two paths to extraordinary outcomes. One of them barely exists.**

- You can try to become the best in the world at a single thing. That path produces NBA players, platinum artists, and Olympic medalists.

- The second path is quieter and far more reliable. Become very good at multiple things. Not elite. Not world class. Just meaningfully better than average.

- This idea is articulated most clearly by the late Scott Adams. Being top 1% in anything is nearly impossible. Being top 25% in several things is achievable for most people. When those skills overlap, you become rare.

- **Rarity comes from overlap, not dominance.**
    
    - There are 2 layers:
        - First is the cornerstone. These are the skills that define what you actually produce. They shape your output. Writing plus humor. Engineering plus systems thinking. Design plus taste.
        - Second is commercialization. These are the skills that turn output into leverage. Business literacy. Distribution. Work ethic. Risk tolerance. Without these, uniqueness stays invisible. Even regularly posting on X is a form of commercialization.

- **Capitalism rewards things that are rare and valuable. You become rare by combining skills that rarely coexist in the same person.**

- AI dramatically lowers the cost of doing this. It makes adjacent skills accessible. Coding, writing, analysis, design, research. The barrier to stacking just collapsed.

- This is why the future does not belong to narrow specialists defending titles. It belongs to people who deliberately build combinations.
