the [streetlight effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Streetlight_effect)—the tendency to search for things we want only where it is easiest to look. The name “streetlight effect” comes from the joke about a policeman finding a drunk looking for his keys beneath a streetlight at night, because that’s where he can see, even though he says he lost them in the park.


The Medici Effect
	- "When you step into the intersection of domains, disciplines or cultures, you must be prepared to fight a battle. The fight can happen on many levels, people may not believe in what you're doing and will make their doubts crystal clear. Sometimes an intersectional idea can threaten an established field, those within that field will naturally do whatever they can to prevent your idea from becoming accepted innovation. You will need to stand up to those within the established field, break away and create your own.”
