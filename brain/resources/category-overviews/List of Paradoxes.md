#### Key Details

- When you begin to look for them, paradoxical truths are found everywhere...
    - The most authentic marketing never feels like marketing.
    - The most authentic salesperson never feels like a salesperson.
    - The most authentic and incredible art feels simple and obvious.

- We even [recognize and label some of these most recurring paradoxes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_paradoxes):
    - the paradox of choice (limiting options, increase choice)
    - Catch-22 (someone is in need of something that can only be had by not being in need of it),
    - Schrödinger's box (also known as Schrödinger's cat; which says that until something is definitely observed, all possible outcomes exist at once),
    - the observer effect (The outcome of an event or experiment is influenced by the presence of the observer)
    - the paradox of cults (cults actually accelerate the sense of individualism, within cohesive communities), and many more...
    - [The Dumb Idea Paradox](https://www.notion.so/The-Dumb-Idea-Paradox-bf85c06c128e4129bad0159e99799d9b?pvs=21)
    - **Paradox of Innovation**
        - “A paradox of innovation and mastery is that breakthroughs often occur when you start down a road, but wander off for a ways and pretend as if you have just begun.”
        - "This illustrates one of the paradoxes of innovation work. Innovation teams know that failure is essential for innovation, yet human nature makes them reluctant to try things that might fail. Moreover, good innovation workers need the stimulation of learning new things in order to feel engaged with their work. Innovation teams need to be forced into doing things they don't yet know how to do to keep their members happy and engaged and to be effective at innovation." ― Vaughn Tan, The Uncertainty Mindset, p. 95
        - "Innovation requires the paradoxical ability to be simultaneously creative and nondelusional about the world; to see what's actually there but also to actively imagine how that shapes the range of meaningful, not-yet-existent, possible outcomes. This requires both making sense of things as they are and also being able to break that framework of sensemaking and interpretation to allow room for the new." ― Vaughn Tan, The Uncertainty Mindset, p. 228
    - **Paradox of Experience**
        - _"[...] the best experiences are both novel and familiar at the same time. That's something of a paradox. But it's also kind of like how we like what we like, but we always appreciate spicing it up a bit so that it's a little more new to us." - Dr. John Maeda_
    - **Paradox of Value/Simplicity**
        - In many creative pursuits - writing, art, marketing, for example - when something is as simple and concise as it can be, it gives off the impression that anyone can do it.
        - [[David Perell]] extended this idea:
            - "Your work is done when it looks so simple that the consumer thinks they could have done it, which means they won’t appreciate how hard you worked."
        - The value is in all of the effort and work it took years ahead of time to achieve that result. And so, if you have never undertaken the effort yourself, you don't completely value the work.
